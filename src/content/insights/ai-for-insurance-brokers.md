Running an insurance brokerage is a constant fight against paperwork. Every new client means a discovery call, a needs analysis, three to five policy comparisons, documentation, and a quote — before you've written a single line of business. Once the policy is placed, the renewal cycle starts again.

Brokers pulling ahead right now aren't doing less of that work. They're using AI to handle the parts that don't require their expertise, so they can spend time on what does: reading clients, structuring complex covers, handling claims disputes. Here's where the wins actually are.

## The admin that eats broker time

Ask any principal broker in a 3-10 person firm where the week goes, and the answer is almost always the same: quote prep, client onboarding paperwork, chasing renewals, and drafting routine correspondence. None of this requires a broker's license — but in most firms, licensed brokers are still doing it.

A rough benchmark: in a mid-size general insurance firm, the average client onboarding — from first call to issued policy — takes 4-6 hours of admin across the team. For a firm writing 15 new policies a month, that's 60-90 hours of admin work that AI can meaningfully compress.

## Quote preparation and comparison

This is the single biggest time drain — and the clearest AI win. Pulling policy details from multiple providers, building a comparison table, and formatting a client-ready proposal is exactly the kind of structured, text-heavy task AI handles well.

A 6-broker commercial insurance firm in Sydney piloted an AI-assisted quoting workflow in early 2026. They fed an AI assistant their standard discovery form responses and provider PDFs. Within four weeks, quote prep time dropped from 3.5 hours per client to under 50 minutes. The AI extracted key cover details, flagged exclusions, drafted the comparison table, and pre-wrote recommendation paragraphs — the broker reviewed and signed off. Same output, a fraction of the time.

## Renewals: where brokers quietly leak revenue

The renewal book is the lifeblood of any brokerage, and most firms manage it with a spreadsheet and manual emails. A client who doesn't hear from you 60 days out is already shopping.

AI-assisted CRM sequences change this without adding headcount. Set up once in HubSpot or a similar platform with AI-written email templates tailored to each policy type, and your renewal outreach runs automatically — personalised by name, policy type, expiry date, and recent claims history. What used to take 90 minutes of manual email-writing per client batch now takes 10 minutes to review and approve. One 8-broker firm in Melbourne added this in Q1 2026 and lifted their renewal contact rate from 41% to 84% in one quarter. Same staff; the AI handled the cadence.

## Documents, SOAs, and routine correspondence

Any document that follows a repeatable structure is a candidate for AI drafting. Statements of advice, cover letters, declination letters, renewal summaries — these are template-driven, and a good AI assistant trained on your firm's past documents can produce a 90% complete draft in under two minutes. A compliance document that used to take 60 minutes to write from scratch takes 15 with AI — the broker reviews and adjusts rather than starting cold.

The rule in a regulated industry: AI drafts, a licensed broker approves. That's not a limitation; it's the workflow. The saving is in not staring at a blank page.

## What AI won't do for you

Be clear-eyed about this. AI can't build the client relationship that keeps a book of business sticky through claims. It can't read the room on a complex commercial risk or judge whether a client's operations have changed enough to warrant a coverage restructure. It won't catch the obscure exclusion that matters after an incident — that's what a skilled broker is for.

The point is to protect time for those things by offloading the things that don't require them.
